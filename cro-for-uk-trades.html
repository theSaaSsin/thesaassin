The 5 leaks we find on 90% of UK trades pages

1. Phone number is hidden or in the wrong place

Visitors arriving from Google searched a problem ("emergency plumber Manchester"). They want to call NOW. If your phone number is buried in the footer or only appears on the contact page, you've lost them. Phone needs to be sticky at the top, in big text, clickable on mobile.

2. Hero says what you DO not what you DELIVER

"Manchester electrician — 25 years' experience" tells the visitor what you are. Better: "Manchester's most-called electrician — quote in 60 seconds, on-site within 24h." Same business, completely different conversion.

3. Trust block is missing or buried

You have 4.9★ from 127 reviews. £2M public liability insurance. NICEIC certified. 25 years in business. None of it is doing any work if it's not in the first 200px above the fold. The single highest-converting trades page format puts trust signals literally next to the phone number.

4. Service tiles instead of one obvious next action

Six tiles for "rewiring", "fuse boards", "EV chargers", "PAT testing" etc. — looks comprehensive, converts terribly. The visitor came with one specific need. They don't want a menu — they want to call you about THEIR problem. Replace the tile grid with one sticky "Get a free quote — 60 seconds" form or phone CTA.

5. No urgency or specificity in the CTA

"Get a quote" works less well than "Get a quote — quotes back within 2 hours" or "Book a slot — next available Tue 7pm". Trades is an urgency business. Build it into the copy.
