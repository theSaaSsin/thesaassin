The 6 leaks we find on most Shopify product pages

1. Hero photo isn't lifestyle, it's catalog

Stock white-background product shots are a 2015 standard. Conversion-optimised stores in 2026 lead with lifestyle (product in use, on a person, in context). White-background shots stay — but as supporting images 2-5, not the hero.

2. Above-the-fold is missing trust + urgency

Product page hero should pack 5 elements above the fold: lifestyle image, product name, price, primary trust signal (★ rating, reviews count), urgency cue (low stock, fast shipping, or limited offer). Most pages we audit have 2-3 of these.

3. Reviews are buried at the bottom

50%+ of buyers scroll straight to reviews before adding to cart. If reviews are below the variant picker and the long product description, you're forcing every buyer through the slow path. Move review summary (★★★★★ 4.8 from 247 reviews) up, right under the price.

4. Variant picker friction kills conversion

Drop-downs labelled "Size" and "Color" with vague abbreviations ("M", "L"). Buyers bounce because they can't see what M or L means in inches. Replace with visual swatches + size guides inline.

5. Shipping costs surprise the buyer at checkout

Shopify's analytics consistently show abandonment spikes at the shipping step. Solution: declare shipping clearly above the fold ("Free UK shipping over £40 · Pay £4.99 below"). Removes the surprise.

6. Add to Cart and Buy Now are blended into the page

The CTA button needs to be the single most visually dominant element. Same colour as nothing else on the page. Sticky on mobile scroll. Most stores use a CTA that blends with the brand palette — costs them 30-50% conversion.
