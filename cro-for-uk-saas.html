What we typically find on UK SaaS landing pages

1. Hero hook is feature-led, not outcome-led

Most SaaS heroes read: "AI-powered project management for modern teams." That's a feature description. The visitor still doesn't know what THEY get. Strong heroes lead with the outcome and the timeframe: "Cut your weekly status meetings by 80% in 14 days."

2. Social proof missing above the fold

Logos of YC, Sequoia, customer marquees — none of it does anything if it's below the fold. Mobile traffic (60-70% of UK SaaS) never sees it. Move it up.

3. Pricing page treats prospects like an FAQ

Three-column pricing tables with feature checkboxes are a 2014 pattern. They make the prospect do the comparison work. Better: outcome-stated pricing ("£99/mo gets your team to 80% adoption in 30 days") with one obvious recommended plan.

4. Signup-to-onboarding bridge is broken

Most SaaS pages drive a free trial signup, then ghost the user for 24-48 hours. Day 1 retention drops to 30% because the user signs up and forgets. Fix: instant onboarding email + a Loom from the founder + a calendar booking link in the welcome flow.

5. CTA copy is generic

"Sign up free", "Get started", "Try now" — all bland. Specific CTAs convert 30-50% higher. Examples: "Start free — see your first dashboard in 90 seconds", "Get demo — UK Mon-Fri only".
